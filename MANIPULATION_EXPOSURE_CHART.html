üéØ COMMON MANIPULATION TECHNIQUES

Fear Amplification
Exaggerate threats. Repeat worst-case scenarios. Keep you in fight-or-flight.
Example: 24/7 news cycles focused on danger, disease, disaster. Reality is safer than ever, but you'd never know it.
Information Overload
Flood with data. Contradict constantly. Make truth impossible to find.
Example: COVID information changed weekly. Experts disagreed. Confusion led to compliance.
False Dichotomy
Present two options only. Hide the third, fourth, fifth options.
Example: "Either vote for us or the world ends." Reality: 100 other options exist.
Peer Pressure
"Everyone else is doing it." Social proof. FOMO. Exclusion threat.
Example: "Join now or be left behind." Creates urgency and conformity pressure.
Authority Worship
"Trust the experts." "The science is settled." Question nothing.
Example: Can't question doctors, scientists, officials. Even when they're wrong.
Gaslighting
Deny reality. Tell you your perception is wrong. Make you doubt yourself.
Example: "That's not happening." (It is.) "You're imagining it." (You're not.)
Scarcity Creation
"Limited time." "Running out." Create artificial urgency.
Example: Black Friday sales. Limited offers. Creates panic buying of things you don't need.
Division Tactics
Divide and conquer. Create "us vs them." Prevent unity.
Example: Political parties, racial division, generational conflict. Keeps people fighting each other, not the system.
Incremental Acceptance
Small steps. Gradual change. Before you know it, you're far from where you started.
Example: Privacy erosion. Each step seems small. 20 years later, total surveillance is normal.
