🛡️ Police Accountability System

Document your case, protect your rights, seek justice

⚠️ IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (If you just experienced this)

  • Seek medical attention immediately - Document all injuries with photos and medical records
  • DO NOT wash or change clothes - Evidence preservation
  • Get badge numbers and names - Of all officers involved
  • Document witnesses - Names, phone numbers, what they saw
  • File complaint within 72 hours - Time is critical

⚖️ Step 3: Legal Action - Kentucky

IMMEDIATE: File Internal Complaint (72 hours)

  1. Contact the police department's Internal Affairs division
  2. Request complaint forms in writing
  3. File formal complaint with all evidence
  4. Request case number and keep all documentation

Federal Civil Rights Complaint (FBI)

FBI Louisville Office: (502) 263-6000

Civil Rights Division: Report at civilrights.justice.gov

  1. File complaint online or by phone
  2. Provide all evidence (medical records, photos, witness statements)
  3. Request investigation under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (Civil Rights violation)

Civil Lawsuit (42 U.S.C. § 1983)

  1. Find attorney: Kentucky Bar Association Lawyer Referral: (502) 564-3795
  2. Specialization: Civil rights, police misconduct, excessive force
  3. Many work on contingency - No upfront cost, they get paid from settlement
  4. Statute of limitations: 1 year in Kentucky - ACT FAST

Kentucky State Complaint

Kentucky Attorney General: (502) 696-5300

Online: ag.ky.gov

  1. File complaint with Office of Civil Rights Enforcement
  2. Request state investigation

Evidence Checklist

  • Photos of all injuries (multiple angles, timestamp)
  • Medical records and bills
  • Police report (request copy)
  • Witness statements (written and signed)
  • Body camera footage (FOIA request)
  • Dash camera footage (FOIA request)
  • 911 call recordings (FOIA request)
  • Officer disciplinary records (FOIA request)
