Major Character Trait Systems (Analyzed)

1. Myers-Briggs (MBTI)

Created: 1940s (Isabel Myers, Katharine Briggs)

Dimensions: 4 dichotomies = 16 types

Framework:

  • E/I: Extraversion vs Introversion (energy source)
  • S/N: Sensing vs Intuition (information gathering)
  • T/F: Thinking vs Feeling (decision making)
  • J/P: Judging vs Perceiving (lifestyle)

Examples: INTJ, ENFP, ISTJ

Strength: Easy to understand, widely known

Weakness: Binary (you're one OR the other, no spectrum)

2. Big Five (OCEAN)

Created: 1980s-1990s (academic consensus)

Dimensions: 5 continuous spectrums

Framework:

  • O: Openness (curiosity, creativity)
  • C: Conscientiousness (discipline, organization)
  • E: Extraversion (sociability, assertiveness)
  • A: Agreeableness (compassion, cooperation)
  • N: Neuroticism (emotional stability)

Strength: Scientifically validated, spectrum-based

Weakness: Doesn't predict behavior in specific situations

3. Enneagram

Created: Ancient (popularized 1970s by Ichazo/Naranjo)

Dimensions: 9 core types

Framework:

  • 1: Perfectionist/Reformer
  • 2: Helper/Giver
  • 3: Achiever/Performer
  • 4: Individualist/Romantic
  • 5: Investigator/Observer
  • 6: Loyalist/Skeptic
  • 7: Enthusiast/Epicure
  • 8: Challenger/Protector
  • 9: Peacemaker/Mediator

Strength: Deep psychological insight, growth paths

Weakness: Hard to type accurately, spiritual/mystical origins

4. DISC

Created: 1920s (William Marston)

Dimensions: 4 behavioral styles

Framework:

  • D: Dominance (results, control)
  • I: Influence (enthusiasm, relationships)
  • S: Steadiness (patience, teamwork)
  • C: Conscientiousness (quality, accuracy)

Strength: Simple, practical for workplace

Weakness: Only measures behavior, not motivation

5. StrengthsFinder (CliftonStrengths)

Created: 2001 (Gallup/Don Clifton)

Dimensions: 34 talent themes (top 5 identified)

Framework Categories:

  • Executing: Achievement, Discipline, Focus
  • Influencing: Communication, Woo, Command
  • Relationship: Empathy, Harmony, Developer
  • Strategic Thinking: Analytical, Learner, Strategic

Strength: Positive (focuses on strengths not weaknesses)

Weakness: Proprietary (paid assessment), commercial

6. VIA Character Strengths

Created: 2004 (Martin Seligman, Chris Peterson)

Dimensions: 24 character strengths under 6 virtues

Framework (6 Virtues):

  • Wisdom: Creativity, Curiosity, Judgment, Love of Learning, Perspective
  • Courage: Bravery, Perseverance, Honesty, Zest
  • Humanity: Love, Kindness, Social Intelligence
  • Justice: Teamwork, Fairness, Leadership
  • Temperance: Forgiveness, Humility, Prudence, Self-Regulation
  • Transcendence: Appreciation of Beauty, Gratitude, Hope, Humor, Spirituality

Strength: Scientifically validated, free, positive psychology

Weakness: Overlapping strengths, hard to distinguish

7. Holland Codes (RIASEC)

Created: 1970s (John Holland)

Dimensions: 6 career interest types

Framework:

  • R: Realistic (hands-on, mechanical)
  • I: Investigative (analytical, scientific)
  • A: Artistic (creative, expressive)
  • S: Social (helping, teaching)
  • E: Enterprising (persuading, leading)
  • C: Conventional (organizing, detail-oriented)

Strength: Useful for career guidance

Weakness: Focused on interests, not deep character
