Pattern 3: Reality & Memory Communication

üåÄ The "Reality Scramble" Pattern (Gaslighting)

Check if this is happening to you:

  • I feel confused about what's real around this person
  • They say things "never happened" when I remember them
  • I constantly apologize even when I did nothing wrong
  • I feel "crazy" or doubt my own memory
  • I'm told I'm "too sensitive" or "dramatic"
  • After talking to them, I question my own perception
If you checked 3 or more: You're likely experiencing gaslighting. This is a manipulation tactic where someone makes you doubt your own reality.

Pattern 4: Boundary & Control Styles

üéØ The "Moving Goalposts" Pattern

‚úÖ Healthy Boundaries

You: "I did what you asked"

Them: "Thank you!"

Goal achieved = Praise received

‚ùå Moving Goalposts

You: "I did what you asked"

Them: "But you didn't do THIS other thing!"

Goal achieved = New demand appears

What's really happening: Some people move the goalposts on purpose. No matter what you do, it's never enough. They add new demands the moment you meet the old ones. This keeps you feeling like a failure and keeps them in control.
Red Flag: If you can NEVER satisfy someone no matter how hard you try, they may be moving goalposts intentionally. It's not about what you do - it's about keeping you trying.

üõ°Ô∏è What To Do If You Recognize These Patterns

First: You're not alone, and this is NOT your fault.

Immediate Actions:

  1. Trust your feelings - If something feels wrong, it probably is
  2. Document things - Journal or voice record your memories (they can't gaslight written evidence)
  3. Talk to someone safe - Teacher, counselor, trusted adult outside the situation
  4. Don't try to "fix" the manipulator - That's not your job and it won't work
  5. Make a safety plan - Know who to call, where to go if needed

Response Scripts (What to say):

When they say "Just joking!":

"I'm not comfortable with jokes like that."

When they flood you with criticism:

"I can only process one thing at a time. What's the main issue?"

When they say something didn't happen:

"I remember it differently. Let's agree to disagree."

When goalposts move:

"I did what was asked. If there are new requirements, please tell me upfront next time."
